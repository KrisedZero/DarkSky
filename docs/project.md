# Pixel Horror Castle

**Genre:** Top-down pixel-art horror  •  **Platform:** PC (Steam)  •  **Engine:** Godot (GDScript)  •  **Resolution:** 320×180 (pixel-art)  •  **Target FPS:** 60  •  **Language:** English (plans for RU)  •  **Phase:** Prototype  •  **Version:** 0.1

### Overview  
Pixel Horror Castle is a gritty, atmospheric exploration game. The player controls a frightened boy in a medieval castle filled with lurking monsters. Lighting and sound create tension: the lantern gives limited visibility (bright nearby, fading into darkness by a few tiles), footsteps and heartbeat cues signal danger, and hiding under furniture or running to lit balconies are key survival tactics. Resources (lamp oil, food, keys, ammo) are scarce and must be managed. Each playthrough involves navigating 7 randomly generated floors of rooms and corridors, finding stairs to advance, and ultimately reaching the rooftop for a final quiet scene and credits. The main menu features animated rain and a moonlit forest (setting the mood).  

### Core Pillars  
- **Exploration & Atmosphere:** Rich pixel-art environments, varied rooms (dungeons, libraries, bedrooms), and dynamic 2D lights/shadows build immersion.  
- **Fear & Stealth:** Monsters patrol; the player can extinguish the lantern to hide, crouch under furniture, or flee. A heartbeat sound and screen “red pulse” indicate a monster chase, intensifying the horror.  
- **Resource Management:** Limited lamp oil (10 min duration) and scarce supplies force careful play. Food (apple, cookie, pie, cheese) restores energy. Treasure-sense potions hint at nearby loot. Gold from chests (1–10 coins, where 3–5 are most common; 10 is very rare) is spent on upgrades.  
- **Progression:** Procedural floors with a blend of safe and dangerous zones. Each floor has a save point (upon climbing stairs). The final win is sitting on the rooftop edge watching the moon; an achievement “Bloodbringer” awaits for finishing the harder “Blood Codex” mode.
